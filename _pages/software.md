Most of the code I write as a computational scientist is one-off analysis code, mostly in Python. Whenever I think a method could be useful to others, I try to share it with the scientific community.
You can find my open-source contributions on <u><a href="https://github.com/martinvoegeleJ">my GitHub profile</a></u>.

Here I highlight some of the bigger projects that I particularly care about.

<!---
--->

## PENSA

_Protein Ensemble Analysis_, a toolbox for exploratory analysis and comparison of structural ensembles of proteins (<u><a href="https://github.com/drorlab/pensa">view on GitHub</a></u>).


## ATOM3D 
(with Raphael Townshend, Patricia Suriana, Alexander Derry)

A benchmark suite for machine learning on three-dimensional molecular structure. Check it out on <u><a href="https://www.atom3d.ai/">atom3d.ai</a></u> or on its <u><a href="https://github.com/drorlab/atom3d">GitHub page</a>.</u>


## Binless WHAM
(with Alfredo Jost-López and Lukas Stelzl)

An efficient implementation of the binless WHAM method for statistical reweighting of biased simulations (<u><a href="https://github.com/bio-phys/binless-wham">view on GitHub</a></u>).
